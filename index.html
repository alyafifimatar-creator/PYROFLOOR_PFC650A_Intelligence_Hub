🔄Functional Principle — Transport Cycle
1

Simultaneous Advance Stroke

All grate lanes advance simultaneously — entire clinker bed moves toward the cooler discharge end.

2

Sequential Return Stroke

Lanes divided into 3 groups; each group retracts individually. No two adjacent lanes withdraw simultaneously — static friction holds clinker in place.

3

Net Forward Transport

The differential stroke produces a net clinker flow toward discharge. Flow regulators maintain constant aeration independent of bed depth variations.

4

Roller Crusher Discharge

Cooled clinker falls onto 4-roller crusher. Fine fractions pass through; lumps are comminuted before clinker discharge transport.

📡 Sensing & Control Infrastructure
Radar Sensors (Clinker Bed Height)

Mounted via antenna extensions through the housing top section. Horn antenna protrudes into ceiling. Protection-air purge prevents dust fouling. Each lane in the inlet zone has a dedicated radar for real-time bed height feedback to PFCC.

Temperature Sensors (Under-cassette)

Mounted directly beneath angle sections welded into the cassette. Sensor surface must maintain permanent contact. Flexible sensor line allows for lane motion. Alarm at 150 °C, trip at 250 °C.

Measure chamber pressure (p1), sub-cassette (p2), and sub-clinker (p3). Combined evaluation infers bed height, granulometry, temperature, and cassette flow resistance — essential for aeration controller tuning.

🔁Stroke Kinematics

Forward stroke: all lanes advance simultaneously. Return stroke: sequential by group (3 groups, no adjacent lanes retract together). Return is faster than advance. Net effect: positive clinker transport with minimal back-transport.

80–350 mm stroke 0–10 str/min Group sequencing Way-encoder feedback
🔩Flow Regulator — Pressure-Balancing Mechanism
Components
  • Housing with bandage and flanges (1)
  • Plate with guiding bush (2)
  • Axle — guides plate and spring (3)
  • Yoke — fixes axle and plate home position (6)
  • Helical spring, ~15 mm preload (5)
  • Cut-outs in bandage sized per flow rate
Pressure Zones
  • p1 — Chamber pressure (below flow regulator)
  • p2 — Pressure below cassette (above plate)
  • p3 — Pressure below clinker bed
  • p4 — Pressure above clinker bed
  • p1–p2 differential drives plate upward
  • Spring force limits and stabilises plate position
Self-Regulation Logic
  • Bed height drops → p below bed decreases → plate rises
  • Spring compression increases → equilibrium restores flow
  • Working range: ~200 mm plate stroke
  • 8 standard flow rates: 500–2,250 Nm³/h
  • Outside working range → flow becomes uncontrolled
  • Orifices limit peak flow on air-clinker fountain events

Preventive Maintenance Program

Structured PM schedule derived from OEM inspection list (BA 841-5123) and PFCC documentation, supplemented with reliability-centred best practices.

🔑Critical Maintenance Principles
  • All grate works require LOTO — hydraulic main switches to zero, main fuse isolated, written countersigned permit
  • Welding directly on cooler structure is prohibited unless specified assembly welds
  • Welding current must never flow through bearings, cylinders, pumps, or control valves (arc damage)
  • Inspect hydraulic oil for water contamination before every restart after a long standstill (condensation)
  • After 50 operating hours (new plant): re-tighten all connection screws
  • After 200 operating hours: comprehensive check of screw unions, oil levels, cylinders, seals, interlockings
  • Stuffing boxes must be re-tightened during run-in and whenever clinker dust passage is observed
  • Carry spare hardfaced roller for annual replacement of most-loaded crusher roller
  • Keep shift book protocols for every maintenance activity
  • Perform all works only with plant cooled down and all safety measures in place
